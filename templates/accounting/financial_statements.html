What are Financial Statements?

Financial statements are the formal, structured records of a business's financial activities and position — comprising the Profit & Loss Account (Income Statement), Balance Sheet, Statement of Changes in Equity (SOCIE), Cash Flow Statement, and Notes to Accounts. They are required for filing income tax returns, applying for loans, conducting statutory audits, satisfying ROC filings, and attracting investors.

VITTAX prepares GAAP-compliant (Indian GAAP / Ind AS as applicable) financial statements for companies and LLPs AND Guidance note compliant financial statements for partnerships, proprietorships, and trusts. Our team ensures all disclosures are complete, schedules are properly annexed, and the financials truly reflect the economic reality of your business.

For companies, we also prepare financials in the prescribed Schedule III format under the Companies Act, 2013, ready for board adoption and statutory audit. For XBRL filers, we prepare XBRL-tagged financials as required by MCA.

⚠️ Incomplete disclosures or non-compliant formats can result in audit qualifications, ROC rejections, and loan application failures.

FAQs

Do you prepare financials for the auditor or after audit?

Both. We prepare draft financials for the auditor to audit, and also incorporate post-audit adjustments for final statements.

What if my books have not been maintained properly?

We offer a "reconstruction" service — rebuilding books from available bank statements, invoices, and records before preparing financials.

Can you prepare XBRL-tagged financials for MCA filing?

Yes. Our XBRL team prepares and validates XBRL-tagged financials for companies required to file XBRL with MCA.
