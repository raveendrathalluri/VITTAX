What is FEMA Compliance?

The Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999 (FEMA) governs all foreign exchange transactions in India — including Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) into Indian companies, Overseas Direct Investment (ODI) by Indian entities abroad, External Commercial Borrowings (ECB), and cross-border asset transfers. FEMA is administered by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) through its Authorised Dealer banks and Regional Offices.

FEMA compliance involves filing mandatory reports (FC-GPR for FDI receipt, FC-TRS for share transfer, ODI returns for overseas investments), obtaining prior RBI approval for restricted transactions, and maintaining prescribed documents for RBI inspection. Non-compliance attracts penalties of up to 3 times the amount involved or ₹2 lakh per day — making this one of the most consequential compliance areas for cross-border businesses.

FAQs

What is the FLA return and who must file it?

The Annual Return on Foreign Liabilities and Assets (FLA) must be filed by July 15 by every Indian company/LLP that has received FDI or made overseas investment — even if no new transactions occurred during the year. Non-filing attracts a flat penalty.

Can FEMA contraventions be regularised?

Yes — through RBI's Compounding mechanism. We assist with the compounding application, calculation of penalty, and representation before RBI.
