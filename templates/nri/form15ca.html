What is Form 145/146 [old-Form 15CA / 15CB]?

Form 145 [old-Form15CA] is a self-declaration submitted online by the remitter (person sending money outside India) to the Income Tax Department, confirming that taxes applicable on the foreign remittance have been deducted or that the remittance is not taxable. Form 146 [old-Form 15CB] is a certificate issued by a Chartered Accountant confirming the nature of the remittance, applicable DTAA provisions, and TDS rates — required for certain categories of remittance.

As per Rule 220 [old Rule 37BB] of the Income Tax Rules and Section 393 [old- section 195] of the Income Tax Act, Form 145/146 [old-Form 15CA/15CB] is mandatory for most foreign remittances above ₹5 lakh per year (with certain exemptions). Banks require these forms before processing the wire transfer. Failure to obtain the forms before remittance can lead to penalties under Income Tax Act.

Who Needs Form 145/146 [old-Form 15CA/15CB]?

✔ Importers & Businesses: Businesses paying for imports, royalties, software, professional services from abroad
✔ Dividend Remittances: Companies remitting dividends to foreign shareholders (NRI/FPI)
✔ Individuals: Individuals sending money to NRI relatives for property purchase or gift
✔ NRI Repatriation: NRIs repatriating money from NRO accounts to their foreign accounts
✔ Loan Repayments: Companies paying foreign loan repayments, interest, or fees
