What is E-Commerce GST Compliance?

E-commerce sellers face a unique set of GST compliance obligations — mandatory GST registration from the first sale (no turnover threshold), TCS (Tax Collected at Source) deduction by marketplace operators, reconciliation of marketplace TCS credits against your GST portal ledger, and special reporting requirements in GSTR-8 (filed by the marketplace, affecting your ITC). Non-compliance — including mismatched TCS credits, unreconciled marketplace sales, or delayed ITR filing — can result in notices, demands, and marketplace account suspension.

At VITTAX, we manage the complete GST compliance lifecycle for e-commerce sellers — from registration across multiple states (if needed), to monthly return preparation based on marketplace sales reports, TCS reconciliation, and annual return filing — whether you sell on Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho, Myntra, Nykaa, your own website, or any combination.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is GST mandatory for e-commerce sellers regardless of turnover?

Yes. Under GST law, every person supplying goods or services through an e-commerce operator must be registered under GST — regardless of turnover. Even if you sell goods worth ₹10,000 per year on Amazon, you need a GSTIN.

What is TCS under GST and how does it affect me?

Marketplace operators (Amazon, Flipkart, etc.) are required to collect TCS at 1% of net sales value from sellers and deposit it with the government. This TCS appears in your electronic cash ledger on the GST portal and can be used to set off your GST liability. VITTAX ensures your TCS credits are correctly reconciled every month.
