What is GST Invoicing Setup?

A GST-compliant invoice is not just a billing document — it is a legal instrument that determines your customer's right to claim Input Tax Credit. A non-compliant invoice (missing GSTIN, wrong HSN/SAC code, incorrect tax rate split) can result in ITC rejection for your buyers and notices for your business. Getting your invoicing system right from the start is critical.

GST Invoicing Setup covers the configuration of your accounting software (Tally Prime, Zoho Books, QuickBooks, or any ERP) for correct GST treatment — including HSN/SAC code mapping for all your products/services, tax rate configuration, invoice templates compliant with GST Rules, and e-invoice setup for businesses liable under the e-invoicing mandate (currently applicable to businesses with turnover above ₹5 crores).

VITTAX also provides training to your accounts team on correct invoicing practices, credit note issuance, and maintenance of GST-required records — so your entire invoicing workflow is compliant and audit-proof from day one.

⚠️ A non-compliant invoice can result in ITC rejection for your buyers and notices for your business.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is e-invoicing and is it mandatory for me?

E-invoicing is the system where B2B invoices are generated through the Invoice Registration Portal (IRP) and assigned a unique Invoice Reference Number (IRN). It is currently mandatory for businesses with aggregate turnover above ₹5 crores in any financial year. VITTAX checks your applicability and sets up the entire system.

What happens if I issue a non-GST-compliant invoice?

Your buyer cannot claim ITC on non-compliant invoices. You may also face notices from the GST department for incorrect reporting. Common errors include missing GSTIN, wrong HSN code, incorrect CGST/SGST/IGST split, and absence of place of supply.

Which accounting software does VITTAX support?

We work with Tally Prime, Zoho Books, QuickBooks Online, Busy Accounting, and custom Excel-based systems. We also assist with migration from one software to another.
