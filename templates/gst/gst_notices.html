What is GST Notice Handling?

The GST department issues various types of notices — ASMT-10 (discrepancy notice after return scrutiny), DRC-01 (show cause notice for demand), GSTR-3A (non-filing notice), REG-17 (cancellation notice), and others. Each notice has a specific response deadline — typically 15 to 30 days — and non-response or inadequate response results in ex-parte orders, confirmed demands, and recovery proceedings against your business.

GST litigation covers the entire legal and administrative journey — from responding to the initial notice, appearing before the Adjudicating Authority, filing an appeal before the GST Appellate Authority (First Appeal), and further appealing to the Appellate Tribunal (GST-AT) or High Court if necessary. Each stage has strict timelines and procedural requirements that require expert legal and tax knowledge.

VITTAX provides expert GST notice response and litigation support — our team reviews your notice, prepares a fact-based written response with supporting documents and legal citations, appears before GST officers on your behalf, and represents you at every level of the appellate hierarchy if needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many days do I have to respond to a GST notice?

Response timelines vary: ASMT-10 — typically 30 days. DRC-01 — 30 days from service. GSTR-3A — 15 days. REG-17 — 7 days. Missing the deadline results in ex-parte orders. VITTAX ensures timely response to every notice.

Can a GST demand be reduced or eliminated at the appeal stage?

Yes. A significant percentage of GST demands are reduced or eliminated at the First Appeal stage if the original notice was issued on incorrect legal grounds or insufficient evidence. VITTAX's litigation team has strong experience in winning relief at the appeal stage.
