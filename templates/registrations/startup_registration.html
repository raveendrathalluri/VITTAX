What is Startup India Registration?

Startup India is a flagship initiative of the Government of India, launched on January 16, 2016, with the objective of building a strong ecosystem for innovation and entrepreneurship in India. The programme is implemented by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT). Registering on the Startup India portal and obtaining recognition as a "Startup" under the DPIIT definition is the first step toward accessing a wide range of government benefits exclusively available to recognised startups.

To be eligible, a startup must: be incorporated as a Private Limited Company, LLP, or Registered Partnership Firm; be less than 10 years old from the date of incorporation; have an annual turnover not exceeding ₹100 crore in any previous financial year; and be working toward innovation, development, or improvement of products/processes/services with high potential for employment generation or wealth creation.

VITTAX handles the Startup India registration application end-to-end — including drafting the innovation description (the most critical part of the application), compiling supporting evidence, and tracking the application to approval. Our application success rate is above 95% because we know exactly how DPIIT evaluates innovation descriptions.

⚠️ Incorporation alone does not make you a DPIIT-recognised startup — you must apply, demonstrate innovation, and receive an official recognition certificate.

Benefits of Startup India / DPIIT Recognition

Tax Benefits

  • • Section 80-IAC — 100% income tax exemption on profits for 3 consecutive years (out of first 10 years)
  • • Section 54GB — Capital gains exemption when individuals invest sale proceeds into a recognised startup

Regulatory Benefits

  • • Self-certification under 9 labour laws (no inspector visits for 3–5 years)
  • • Self-certification under 3 environment laws (for green-category industries)
  • • Fast-track exit mechanism under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (90-day wind-up)

Intellectual Property Benefits

  • • 80% rebate on patent filing fees + fast-track examination under Startup Patent Program
  • • 50% rebate on trademark filing fees (TM-A form)

Funding & Network

  • • Access to Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS) — up to ₹50 lakh grants/soft loans
  • • SIDBI Fund of Funds — government-backed VC funds investing in recognised startups
  • • GeM portal — startups can list products/services without prior turnover/experience requirement

Frequently Asked Questions

Is every new company automatically a "Startup" under DPIIT?

No — incorporation alone does not make you a DPIIT-recognised startup. You must apply on the Startup India portal, demonstrate that your business is innovative, and receive an official recognition certificate from DPIIT. The recognition is not automatic.

What makes a business "innovative" for DPIIT purposes?

DPIIT looks for one of three criteria: (a) work toward development or improvement of a product/process/service with high employment or wealth creation potential; (b) scalable business model with high potential; or (c) use of technology or IP in a novel way. VITTAX drafts innovation descriptions that clearly establish this for your specific business.

Can a business older than 10 years apply for Startup India recognition?

No — the entity must be less than 10 years old from its date of incorporation. Additionally, it must not have been formed by splitting or reconstructing an existing business.
