What is NGO-Darpan Registration?

NGO-Darpan is an online interface portal developed by NITI Aayog in collaboration with the National Informatics Centre (NIC) to maintain a database of voluntary organisations and NGOs in India. It serves as the central repository of information about civil society organisations — their registration details, area of work, financial information, and governance — enabling the government, donors, and the general public to verify the authenticity and track record of an NGO before engaging with it.

NGO-Darpan registration (obtaining a unique Darpan ID) is mandatory for any NGO, trust, society, or Section 8 company seeking: (a) government grants from central government ministries or departments; (b) CSR funding from corporate donors (as the MCA requires corporate CSR committees to verify NGO credentials through Darpan); and (c) FCRA registration for receiving foreign contributions. From 2021, the MHA requires all FCRA applicants to furnish their NGO-Darpan unique ID as part of the FCRA application.

Beyond mandatory compliance, a Darpan ID signals credibility — it demonstrates that the NGO has undergone a basic identity verification by NITI Aayog, making donors and government grant-makers more confident in the organisation. VITTAX manages the Darpan registration including uploading annual reports, financial statements, and governance data as required by the portal.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Darpan registration the same as FCRA registration?

No — they are separate. Darpan registration gives you a unique NGO identity number on the NITI Aayog portal. FCRA registration (from MHA) gives you the legal permission to receive foreign contributions. However, from 2021, Darpan ID is mandatory to file for FCRA registration. You need Darpan first, FCRA second.

Is Darpan ID mandatory for CSR funds?

While not legally mandated by statute, the CSR rules under the Companies Act (Schedule VII) require companies to verify NGO credentials before releasing CSR funds. Most corporate CSR committees require a Darpan ID as part of their due diligence. Many government CSR portals also require Darpan ID for NGO listing.

Can a newly registered NGO with no financial history get a Darpan ID?

Yes — there is no minimum age or financial track record requirement for Darpan registration. New NGOs can register immediately after obtaining their Trust Deed or Section 8 COI and PAN.
