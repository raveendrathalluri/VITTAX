What is GS1 Barcode Registration?

A barcode is a machine-readable representation of data (typically a product's identity and price) used for automatic identification and data capture (AIDC) at retail checkout counters, warehouses, and e-commerce fulfilment centres. GS1 is the global non-profit organisation that administers the international barcode numbering system — the EAN-13 barcode (used in India and internationally) and the UPC-A barcode (used in the USA/Canada) are both GS1 standards.

GS1 India is the country chapter of GS1 Global, operating under the Quality Council of India. When you register with GS1 India, you receive a unique Global Company Prefix (GCP) — a 7–9 digit company-specific number that forms the base of all your product barcodes (GTINs — Global Trade Item Numbers). Each product SKU (size/colour/variant) gets its own unique GTIN, encoded into the barcode printed on the product label.

GS1 barcodes are the only barcodes accepted by major Indian retailers (D-Mart, Reliance Retail, Big Bazaar, Spencer's), all leading e-commerce platforms (Amazon India, Flipkart — Amazon specifically requires GS1 GTINs for brand-owner listings), and international export markets. Using unofficial or random barcodes downloaded from non-GS1 sources can result in product rejection by retailers and deactivation of listings on e-commerce platforms.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use barcodes I find online or generate using free barcode software?

No — barcodes generated without a GS1-allocated GCP are unofficial and not unique. They can conflict with another company's products in retail systems, leading to incorrect scanning at POS. Amazon and major retailers specifically require GS1-sourced GTINs.

Is one barcode per product or per variant?

One GTIN per SKU — meaning each unique combination of product + size + colour + flavour needs its own barcode. A 500ml and a 1-litre bottle of the same product are two separate GTINs.

Do GS1 barcodes expire?

GS1 membership is renewed annually. As long as your membership is active, your GTINs remain valid. Lapsed membership does not invalidate barcodes already printed, but you cannot generate new GTINs or maintain your GS1 company data.

Can I use one barcode for selling in both India and international markets?

Yes — GS1 GTINs are globally unique. An EAN-13 barcode registered in India is accepted at retailers and e-commerce platforms worldwide. This is a key advantage of GS1 over any non-standard barcode.
