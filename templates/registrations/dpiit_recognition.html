What is DPIIT Recognition?

DPIIT Recognition is the formal acknowledgement by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade that your entity qualifies as a "Startup" under the Government of India's definition. While Startup India Registration covers the portal registration, DPIIT Recognition specifically refers to the official certificate and DPIIT reference number that unlocks the full suite of statutory benefits — particularly the Section 80-IAC tax holiday, Angel Tax exemption, self-certification under labour laws, and access to the Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS).

Many founders confuse completing the Startup India profile with receiving DPIIT recognition. The recognition is an active evaluation by DPIIT of your business's innovative nature — it is not automatic upon registration. The innovation description, evidence of product development, scalability, and differentiation are all assessed. A rejection or delay at this stage can delay access to tax benefits and funding for months.

VITTAX specialises in DPIIT recognition applications — our team has successfully obtained recognition for startups across technology, healthcare, agriculture, fintech, edtech, social impact, and manufacturing sectors. We understand what DPIIT looks for and how to articulate it compellingly and accurately.

⚠️ Completing the Startup India profile is NOT sufficient to claim tax benefits or labour law self-certification — DPIIT Recognition requires an active evaluation and approval.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is DPIIT recognition permanent?

DPIIT recognition remains valid until the startup crosses any of the eligibility thresholds (10 years of age or ₹100 crore turnover). There is no annual renewal. However, the startup must continue to meet the eligibility conditions to avail the benefits.

Can a startup that was rejected for DPIIT recognition reapply?

Yes — a startup can reapply after addressing the reason for rejection (typically an insufficient innovation description). VITTAX reviews rejected applications, identifies gaps, and submits a strengthened reapplication.
