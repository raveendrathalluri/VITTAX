What is a Trade Licence?

A Trade Licence (also called a Business Licence or Municipal Licence) is a certificate issued by the local municipal body — Municipal Corporation, Municipality, Gram Panchayat, or Town Panchayat — authorising a person or entity to carry on a specific trade or business from a particular premises within its jurisdictional limits. It is governed by the respective state's Municipal Acts and local body regulations, and varies in name and procedure from state to state (e.g., "Gumasta" in Maharashtra, "Trade Licence" in West Bengal and Karnataka, "Corporation Licence" in Tamil Nadu).

This license ensures that your business complies with local safety, health, and regulatory standards, helping you operate without legal interruptions. Operating without a valid trade licence attracts monetary penalties, forced closure, and in some states, criminal prosecution of the proprietor or director.

Trade Licences are renewed annually (in most municipalities), and non-renewal attracts late fees and potential cancellation. VITTAX manages trade licence applications, renewals, and amendments for businesses across all major Indian cities and municipal jurisdictions, ensuring that your licence remains valid and correctly reflects your current business activity and address.

Registration Process & Timeline

1

Category Identification

1 day

Identify the correct trade/business category as per the municipal body's licence classification list. Different categories have different fee structures and inspection requirements.

2

Document Preparation

1 day

Compile identity, address, and premises documents; prepare the application form prescribed by the municipal body.

3

Application Filing

1 day

Submit online (on the municipal corporation portal, where available) or in person at the municipal ward office. Pay prescribed fee.

4

Premises Inspection

7–30 days (municipality-dependent)

Municipal inspector visits the premises to verify compliance with zoning, health, sanitation, and safety norms. VITTAX prepares you for inspection.

5

Licence Issuance

3–7 days post-inspection

Upon satisfactory inspection, the Trade Licence is issued. It must be displayed prominently at the business premises at all times.

Documents Required

Identity & Business

  • • PAN card and Aadhaar / identity proof of proprietor / director
  • • Certificate of Incorporation / partnership deed / proprietorship proof
  • • GST Registration Certificate (GSTIN)

Premises

  • • Rent agreement / lease deed (if rented) with NOC from landlord
  • • Property ownership documents (if owned)
  • • Latest property tax receipt of the premises
  • • Building plan / site plan (for manufacturing units)

Other (Where Applicable)

  • • FSSAI licence copy (for food businesses)
  • • Pollution NOC / Consent to Operate (for industries with effluents or emissions)
  • • Fire NOC (for establishments above prescribed size or floor area)

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a Trade Licence the same as a Shop & Establishment registration?

No — they are different registrations issued by different authorities. Shop & Establishment is registered under the state Labour Department and governs working conditions and hours. Trade Licence is issued by the municipal body and governs the right to carry on a specific trade from specific premises. Most businesses need both.

Does a home-based business need a Trade Licence?

It depends on the municipal body. Some municipalities exempt home-based micro enterprises below a certain turnover. Others require a licence even for home businesses. VITTAX checks the applicable municipal rules before advising.

What happens if I change my business activity after getting a Trade Licence?

You must amend the Trade Licence to reflect the new business category before commencing the new activity. Operating under a different trade category than what is licensed is a violation. VITTAX manages amendments as part of our compliance retainer.

How far in advance should I renew my Trade Licence?

Most municipal bodies require renewal before December 31 (for the upcoming calendar year) or before the licence expiry date. Late renewal attracts penalties of 25%–100% of the licence fee in most cities. VITTAX sends reminders 60 days before expiry.
