What are LLP Amendments?

LLPs — like companies — require formal MCA filings for every significant change in their structure. Partner induction or retirement, change in profit-sharing ratio, capital contribution changes, amendment of the LLP Agreement, change in LLP name, or shift in registered office — all must be reported to the MCA within prescribed deadlines. The key forms are: Form 3 (change in LLP Agreement — within 30 days), Form 4 (partner changes — within 30 days), Form 5 (change in name — requires MCA approval), and Form 15 (change in registered office).

Failure to file Form 3 or Form 4 within 30 days attracts an additional fee of ₹100 per day per form. More significantly, an undisclosed partner change — without proper documentation and MCA filing — can create disputes over profit entitlement, liability allocation, and exit rights. VITTAX prepares all LLP amendment documentation professionally and files within statutory deadlines.

⚠️ An undisclosed partner change without MCA filing means the outgoing partner continues to bear legal and financial liability for the LLP's obligations in the eyes of the law.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if a partner leaves without updating MCA records?

Legally, the outgoing partner remains a designated partner in MCA records until Form 4 is filed. This means they continue to bear legal and financial liability for the LLP's obligations in the eyes of the law — a significant personal risk for the departed partner.
